Tips for Driving in New Jersey

Avoiding an accident is ALWAYS the best outcome. Don’t just let someone hit you. You only have one body — protect it.
  1. Patience: Slow down, leave early, and avoid rushing. The goal is to arrive safely, not to set a personal speed record.
  2. Communication: Other drivers cannot guess your intentions. Use your signals, headlights, horn, and brake lights so they understand what you plan to do.
  3. Anticipation: Drive in a predictable manner. Sudden or unusual movements force other drivers to react quickly, increasing the likelihood of a collision.
  4. Visibility: If you can’t see another driver, they can’t see you. Position your vehicle so cross-traffic can see you, and so you can see the brake lights ahead even when following another car.
  5. Large Vehicles: Trucks and buses require much more distance to stop. Never cut into their safety zone, and always give them extra space to maneuver.
