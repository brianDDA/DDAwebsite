How Insurance Companies Decide Your Price

Insurance carriers and their underwriters reward stability, safety, and easy billing. The more you look like a low-risk, easy-to-service client, the more likely you are to qualify for credits and discounts.

Big Picture: Companies like to see continuous coverage, clean claims and driving history, safe vehicles and workplaces, and simple payment methods such as Electronic Funds Transfer (EFT) or pay-in-full.

Commercial Insurance – How Businesses Can Save

For business owners, pricing is heavily influenced by how seriously you take safety and risk management. Many commercial carriers offer better pricing when they see written programs, training records, and proof that you monitor drivers and vehicles.

What Underwriters Like to See on Commercial Accounts

  • Continuous coverage with no unexplained lapses in insurance.
  • Few or no losses, plus documentation showing how you corrected past problems.
  • Clear hiring standards for drivers and employees (background checks, licensing checks).
  • Written safety and fleet policies, signed by employees and enforced consistently.
  • Use of EFT or pay-in-full to reduce billing issues and transaction fees.
  • Prompt reporting of claims and active return-to-work plans for injured employees.

1. Pre-Trip Inspections

Requiring drivers to perform and document pre-trip inspections helps catch problems before they become accidents or roadside breakdowns. Underwriters love seeing:

  • Standardized inspection checklists for every vehicle.
  • Drivers trained on what to look for and how to report defects.
  • Mechanic sign-off that defects are corrected before the vehicle goes back on the road.

2. Preventative Maintenance Programs

A formal preventative maintenance (PM) program shows that you don’t wait for vehicles or equipment to fail. This can reduce claim frequency and support better pricing.

  • Scheduled services at specific mileage or time intervals.
  • Written PM logs and invoices stored for each unit.
  • Policies that vehicles with safety defects are parked until repaired.

3. Driver Education & Ongoing Training

Many carriers provide or recognize driver safety training. Underwriters want:

  • Documented driver orientation for new hires.
  • Annual safety meetings or toolbox talks.
  • Use of defensive driving courses or online fleet training modules.

4. MVR Monitoring & Background Checks

Your drivers are often your biggest exposure. Strong hiring and monitoring practices can make a big difference in premium:

  • Pre-hire Motor Vehicle Reports (MVRs) on all driver candidates.
  • At least annual MVR checks on existing drivers, more often for higher risk fleets.
  • Written rules for how many points, violations, or accidents disqualify a driver.
  • Background checks for positions handling money, keys, or sensitive property.

5. Licensing & Qualifications

Underwriters expect every driver to have the proper license and endorsements for the vehicle they operate.

  • Copies of driver licenses and endorsements kept in HR or safety files.
  • Procedures to pull DMV abstracts when licenses renew.
  • Policies preventing unlicensed or improperly licensed driving “just this once.”

6. Return-to-Work Programs (Workers’ Compensation)

A Return-to-Work (RTW) program brings injured workers back in a light-duty or modified role while they recover. This can:

  • Reduce the cost and length of workers’ compensation claims.
  • Show the carrier you actively manage injuries instead of leaving everything to the system.
  • Improve experience modification factors over time, which can significantly lower premiums.

7. Billing Choices – EFT & Pay-In-Full

Many commercial carriers offer credits for:

  • Pay-in-full (paying the annual premium at once) – often a noticeable discount.
  • EFT / automatic withdrawals – reduces cancellations and avoids some billing fees.
  • Avoiding frequent credit card payments that carry processing costs.
Tip: Ask your agent what you may not be doing that could reduce your premium.

  • OSHA – Motor Vehicle Safety for Employers
  • OSHA – Motor Vehicle Safety (general guidance)
  • FMCSA – Vehicle Inspection, Repair & Maintenance (49 CFR Part 396)

Personal Insurance – How Individuals & Families Can Save

On the personal side, underwriters focus on your driving record, claims history, homeownership, and how you pay your bills. Many discounts are automatic once you qualify, but you often have to ask for them.

What Underwriters Reward on Personal Policies

  • Continuous auto and home coverage with no lapses.
  • Homeownership (even if you only insure your auto with that carrier).
  • Good credit and on-time payments.
  • Preferred payment methods: EFT or pay-in-full instead of frequent card payments.
  • Good student discounts for young drivers with strong grades.
  • Completion of approved defensive driving courses.
  • Telematics or usage-based programs that show safe driving behavior.

1. Continuous Coverage

Insurers may charge more if they see gaps in your insurance history. To save:

  • Avoid letting policies cancel for non-payment.
  • When switching, shop for a new policy at least two weeks before the effective date. Start/end policies on the same date.

2. Homeowner & Multi-Policy Discounts

Many companies offer a discount just for being a homeowner, and another discount if you bundle home and auto.

  • If you rent now but plan to buy, let your agent know when you become a homeowner.
  • Get quotes that show the difference between stand-alone auto vs. bundled home/auto.

3. Billing Choices – EFT vs. Credit Card

The way you pay can affect your premium:

  • Pay-in-full – Many carriers reduce the total cost when you pay the entire term up front.
  • EFT / automatic payments – Often earns a small discount and avoids late fees.
  • Credit cards – Convenient, but may involve transaction fees and usually don’t earn extra discounts.
Quick win: Ask your company if switching from monthly credit card payments to pay-in-full or EFT will reduce your premium.

4. Good Student Discounts

If you have a young driver on the policy, most insurers give a discount when they maintain a “B” average or better. Typically, you’ll need:

  • Recent report cards or transcripts showing grades.
  • Proof of full-time enrollment.
  • Updated documents each year to keep the discount.

5. Defensive Driving / Driver Improvement Courses

Completing an approved defensive driving course can reduce points on your license and earn a premium discount for several years, especially in states like New Jersey. Many carriers accept:

  • Online or classroom defensive driving courses approved by the state motor vehicle agency.
  • Courses offered by AAA, AARP, National Safety Council, or other recognized providers.
  • Certificates submitted to your agent or uploaded to your carrier’s portal.

6. Safe Driving, Vehicle Choice & Deductibles

You can further reduce costs by:

  • Maintaining a clean driving record (no at-fault accidents or tickets).
  • Choosing vehicles with strong safety ratings and common replacement parts (often cheaper to insure).
  • Raising your deductibles if you can comfortably afford them in an emergency.
  • Dropping collision/comprehensive on older vehicles where the coverage may cost more than the car is worth.
