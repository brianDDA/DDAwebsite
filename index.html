Frequently Asked Questions

Personal Insurance FAQs

1. What factors affect my auto insurance premium?
Premiums are influenced by your driving record, age, location, vehicle type, mileage, and coverage limits.

2. What is the difference between actual cash value and replacement cost?
Actual cash value pays the depreciated value of your property; replacement cost pays to replace it with a new item of similar kind and quality.

3. How much homeowners insurance coverage do I really need?
You should insure your home for the cost to rebuild it—not market value. This includes structure, personal belongings, and liability.

4. Does homeowners insurance cover floods or earthquakes?
Typically, no. Flood and earthquake insurance usually require separate policies or endorsements.

5. Will my insurance rates increase if I file a claim?
Possibly. It depends on the claim type, severity, frequency, and insurer policies.

Commercial Insurance FAQs

6. What types of insurance does a small business need?
Most businesses need general liability, commercial property, workers’ compensation, and possibly cyber, commercial auto, and professional liability.

7. What is a Business Owners Policy (BOP)?
A BOP bundles general liability and commercial property coverage, often at a lower cost than purchasing separately.

8. How does workers’ compensation insurance work?
It covers employee injuries or illnesses related to work and provides medical care, lost wages, and liability protection for the employer.

9. What is cyber liability insurance and who needs it?
It protects against risks like data breaches, cyberattacks, and privacy failures. Any business that handles customer data, payments, or online operations benefits from it.

10. How do I determine proper coverage limits for my business?
Coverage depends on business size, industry risks, assets, revenue, contracts, and legal requirements. A risk assessment with a broker can help tailor limits.
